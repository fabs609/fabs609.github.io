The Celestial Sentinel

The Celestial Sentinel is a mysterious, intricately carved artifact resembling a humanoid head, believed to be from an ancient civilization that connected deep-sea exploration with celestial myths. Crafted from an unknown alloy, its surface is covered in cryptic symbols resembling star charts and tidal patterns, suggesting it could have been used for navigation or held ceremonial importance.

Purpose & Theories: Scholars and explorers speculate that the artifact served as a guardian effigy to ward off intruders, a deep-sea navigational device attuned to planetary alignments, or a ritualistic offering to cosmic entities. Its true purpose remains unclear, but its discovery suggests it was never meant to be found.

Origins & Discovery: Recovered from the Erebus Abyss, an uncharted trench known for unusual magnetic activity, the Celestial Sentinel was found amidst gravitational fluctuations and faint luminescent pulses.

Material & Features: Made of a corrosion-resistant metallic stone composite, the artifact is about the size of a human skull but features elongated characteristics and a crown-like ridge. Its surface bears untranslatable glyphs arranged in constellatory patterns. The most unusual feature is its faint hum when exposed to starlight or deep-sea bioluminescence.
