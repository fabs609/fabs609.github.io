Abyssal Metropolis

The Abyssal Metropolis is an ancient, submerged city believed to be a relic of a forgotten civilization that once thrived beneath the ocean's depths. The city is marked by grand, ruined structures made from a mix of coral-encrusted stone and metal alloys resistant to the ocean's extreme conditions. Unusual geometric patterns adorn the walls, with certain areas glowing faintly in the dim light of the deep sea.

Purpose & Theories: Scholars speculate that the city was either a hub of advanced technology and culture, designed for deep-sea habitation, or a sacred location for underwater rituals. Some believe it was a sanctuary built to escape surface conflicts, while others propose that it might have been a research facility dedicated to oceanic life. The true purpose of the Abyssal Metropolis remains an enigma.

Origins & Discovery: The city was discovered in the Mariana Trench, after lying hidden for millennia. Divers exploring the trench reported encountering large, intricately designed structures, seemingly untouched by time, and faint electromagnetic signals emanating from the site before they found the entrance to the ruins.
